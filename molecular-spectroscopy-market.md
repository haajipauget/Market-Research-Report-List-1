<p><p>Molecular spectroscopy encompasses various techniques, each useful for analyzing molecular compositions. NMR (Nuclear Magnetic Resonance) spectroscopy provides insights into molecular structure and dynamics by examining magnetic properties of nuclei. UV-Visible spectroscopy measures light absorption in the ultraviolet and visible regions, allowing for the study of electronic transitions in molecules. Infrared spectroscopy identifies functional groups by measuring molecular vibrations, while Near Infrared spectroscopy focuses on overtones and combinations of vibrations, often used in food and pharmaceutical industries for quality control.</p></p>

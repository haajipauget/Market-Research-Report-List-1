<p><p>The surgery electrodes market comprises various types, each serving specific surgical needs. Needle electrodes are utilized for precise applications, delivering energy directly into tissue, often in minimally invasive procedures. Blade electrodes enable cutting and coagulation through their sharp edges, making them ideal for incisions. Ball electrodes offer a larger surface area for even energy distribution, suitable for larger tissue areas. Hook electrodes are designed for effective tissue manipulation and removal, providing versatility in surgical applications. Each type plays a crucial role in enhancing surgical efficiency and outcomes.</p></p>

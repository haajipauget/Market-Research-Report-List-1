<p><p>Labeled and unlabeled secondary antibodies are crucial components in various immunological assays, including western blots, ELISA, and immunofluorescence. Labeled secondary antibodies are conjugated with detectable markers, such as enzymes or fluorophores, enabling visualization or quantification of the target primary antibody. In contrast, unlabeled secondary antibodies serve as a binding agent without any immediate signal output, often requiring additional steps for detection.</p><p>The market for labeled and unlabeled secondary antibodies is experiencing robust growth driven by increasing research activities in biotechnology, pharmaceuticals, and diagnostics.

<p><p>The reconfigurable robot market features three main architectures: Chain, Lattice, and Hybrid. Chain architecture consists of linked modules that can extend or retract, enabling flexibility in shape and function. Lattice architecture utilizes interconnected nodes, allowing robots to reorganize into various configurations for diverse tasks and environments. Hybrid architecture combines elements from both chain and lattice designs, offering enhanced adaptability and efficiency. Together, these architectures support a wide range of applications in manufacturing, healthcare, and exploration, driving innovation in robotics.</p></p>
